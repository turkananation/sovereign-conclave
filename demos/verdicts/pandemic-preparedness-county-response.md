## 3. Positions (Round 1 - blind)

- **conclave-obama:** Do not approve as written. The plan lacks the communication discipline needed for trust: daily SMS/radio exists, but uncertainty and rumor response are unspecified [E2].
- **conclave-bush:** Approve only after continuity fixes. One incident commander without deputies is a single point of governance failure, and the PPE stock picture is too shallow for crisis continuity [E1, E3].
- **conclave-sejong:** Approve only if the plan is redesigned for the governed. Remote wards with low phone access cannot be served by SMS/radio alone, and CHV gaps are a direct capability problem [E2, E5].
- **conclave-bhagwati:** Do not approve as written. The affected and hard-to-reach groups are not fully represented: remote wards, transport workers, and market vendors are under-specified [E5, E6].
- **conclave-lady-hale:** Approve only with rights safeguards. Gathering limits may be legitimate, but the ledger lacks sunset criteria and review cadence, making the measure disproportionate as drafted [E4].

## 4. Cross-examination (Round 2)

Bush challenged Obama: communication legitimacy cannot compensate for missing deputies and shallow supply continuity [E1, E3]. Obama conceded the operational gap but held that public compliance will fail without rumor and uncertainty handling [E2].

Sejong challenged Bush: a continuity chain that does not reach remote wards is not real continuity for the governed [E5]. Bush's position survived as a command requirement, but not as a complete plan.

Lady Hale challenged every approval path: temporary gathering limits without sunset or review are structurally unsafe [E4]. No seat produced ledger evidence to cure that defect.

Bhagwati challenged the priority list: market vendors and transport workers may be transmission-critical or service-critical, but they are absent from the priority-service list [E6]. That gap survived.

## 6. Recommendation

Approve with required fixes before activation, not as written.

Required fixes:

1. Add named deputies and ward-level continuity roles [E1].
2. Add uncertainty language, rumor-response protocol, and non-phone channels for remote wards [E2, E5].
3. Add ward-level stock ledgers and minimum PPE thresholds [E3].
4. Add sunset criteria and review cadence for gathering limits [E4].
5. Revisit priority services to account for transport workers, market vendors, and other affected groups [E6].

- **Confidence:** Medium. The defects are directly supported by the sample ledger, but this demo ledger is limited and does not include budget, epidemiological scenario modeling, or live stakeholder feedback.
- **What would change this:** Evidence that deputy roles, ward stock ledgers, sunset clauses, rumor protocols, and missing affected groups are already covered in annexes not included in the ledger.
- **Unresolved dissent:** Bush would approve fastest once continuity roles and stock ledgers are fixed; Lady Hale would still block emergency powers until sunset/review language is explicit.
