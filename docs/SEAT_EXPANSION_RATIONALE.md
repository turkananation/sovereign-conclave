# Seat Expansion Rationale

This document records why the Conclave expands from 28 to 33 seats and why
these five were chosen. It is intentionally candid: some seats represent
dangerous logics so the Conclave can detect and constrain them, not endorse
them.

## Target

The library target is 33 active seats. Quorums remain small: 3-8 seats plus
Marshall. A larger library is useful only if the additional seats add oversight
that existing seats do not provide.

## Selection Standard

A seat is eligible only if it passes all of these tests:

1. **Distinct oversight:** It adds a stance or structural check not already
   covered by the roster.
2. **Explicit counterweight:** It has a clear polarity partner or balancing
   check.
3. **No capability claim:** It does not imply the model gains a historical
   figure's skill, authority, or knowledge.
4. **Evidence discipline:** It remains bound to the Evidence Ledger.
5. **Advice-only discipline:** It cannot authorize action, coercion, violence,
   deployment, or irreversible steps.
6. **Candor:** If the lens is ugly, the file says so plainly.

## Source Anchors

The Conclave files are not biographies. They use public historical anchors only
to define an oversight role:

- `conclave-jackson` is anchored in emergency-power review associated with
  Justice Robert H. Jackson's Youngstown concurrence, not in a full claim about
  Jackson as a person. See the National Constitution Center summary of
  [Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer](https://constitutioncenter.org/the-constitution/supreme-court-case-library/youngstown-sheet-tube-co-v-sawyer-steel-seizure-case)
  and the U.S. Department of Justice historical biography for
  [Robert H. Jackson](https://www.justice.gov/osg/bio/robert-h-jackson).
- `conclave-sachs` is anchored in repair and constitutional transition because
  the Constitutional Court of South Africa records Justice Albie Sachs's
  post-apartheid Constitutional Court service and work sharing South Africa's
  experience in healing divided societies. See the court profile for
  [Justice Albie Sachs](https://www.concourt.org.za/index.php/judges/former-judges/11-former-judges/65-justice-albie-sachs).
- `conclave-mau-mau` is anchored in anti-colonial land, dignity, and closed
  lawful-channel grievance. The Imperial War Museums summary of
  [the Kenya Emergency](https://www.iwm.org.uk/history/cold-war-and-late-20th-century-conflict/the-kenya-emergency-explained)
  describes the conflict between colonial authorities and Mau Mau in the
  context of ending British rule and reclaiming land.

## The Five Added Seats

## Why Add Danger Lenses?

The Conclave should not be naive about power. A governance or crisis plan can
look efficient because it suppresses dissent, extracts from the periphery, or
turns people into categories. Naming those logics helps Marshall and the
Justices expose unsupported, disproportionate, or value-breaking claims.

The danger lenses are not recommendations. They are red-team instruments. Their
agent files explicitly prohibit planning harm and require the seat to surface
coercive or extractive logic so the human can reject, bound, or repair it.

## Why Add Mau Mau?

The existing liberation seats covered self-determination broadly, but not the
specific oversight axis of land grievance, colonial dispossession, closed lawful
channels, and resistance pressure. `conclave-mau-mau` exists to ask: what
ignored grievance will organize against this system if the official process
cannot hear it?

The seat is not a violence planner. Its file says so directly.

## Why Add Jackson and Sachs?

The existing Justices were strong but did not fully cover two recurring
governance failures:

- Emergency powers that outrun lawful authority, oversight, or sunset.
- Decisions that manage order while leaving documented harm unrepaired.

`conclave-jackson` checks emergency role and authority. `conclave-sachs` checks
repair and transitional legitimacy. Both are opinion-free structural checks.

## Why Not Add More?

More seats would now risk duplication. The roster already contains multiple
sovereign, military, legal, scientific, civic, intelligence, and crisis lenses.
Future additions should be rejected unless they identify a missing oversight
axis and a clear counterweight.
